Chimps Drink Fermented Fruit Juice. Now Scientists Think That’s Why You Drink Beer.

What’s going on:

A new study published this week found that chimpanzees actively seek out and consume fermented fruit juice in the wild, metabolizing alcohol at a surprisingly efficient rate. Researchers analyzed chimp urine — the scientific method, glamorous as ever — and found consistent evidence of alcohol consumption as a habitual behavior, not just an accidental byproduct of eating overripe fruit. The implication: our relationship with alcohol may predate humanity itself, stretching back millions of years along our shared primate lineage.

The takeaway:

Scientists now have evidence that reaching for a drink after a long, hard week is not a modern coping mechanism. It is, apparently, ancient primate tradition. The chimps of the African forest have been doing this for millions of years, before there were wars, markets, spy cells, or polar vortices to worry about. In the entire week’s news — Iran, Ecuador, Texas primaries, oil prices, microplastics — the most relatable beings on the planet may be the ones sitting in the forest, quietly sipping fermented juice. We were always going to end up here.
