The Big Story

Khamenei Is Dead — Day 2 of the US-Iran War

What’s going on:

On the second day of Operation Epic Fury, Israel confirmed the killing of Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, along with the commander of the Revolutionary Guard, Iran’s defense minister, and a top security council adviser. Trump called Khamenei “one of the most evil people in history” and said heavy bombing would “continue uninterrupted throughout the week.” Iran is preparing for Khamenei’s funeral while vowing to strike the US and Israel “with a force they have never experienced before.” Protests erupted in Pakistan and Iraq — at least 9 killed when crowds breached the US consulate in Karachi.

The takeaway:

Killing a sitting head of state is a line most wars never cross. Whether this destabilizes Iran’s regime or hardens it is the central question now. Iran’s leadership is hobbled but not gone — and a cornered government with nuclear ambitions and proxy forces across the region is its own kind of danger. The Strait of Hormuz, through which ~20% of the world’s oil passes, is now openly being discussed as a potential Iranian chokepoint. The next 72 hours matter more than the next 72 days.
