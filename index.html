The Big Story

Tehran Burns on Day 2 — No Successor, No Off-Ramp

What’s going on:

A massive explosion rocked Tehran on Sunday morning as Israeli forces said they were targeting the “heart” of the city after declaring they had “cleared the path to Tehran” on Day 1. Khamenei — who had no designated successor — was confirmed dead by Iranian state television. Iran’s retaliation continued: Qatar, Kuwait, and Bahrain all intercepted missile barrages. Oman, which had served as a back-channel between Iran and the US during nuclear talks, reported a drone attack on its Duqm port — a key US Navy logistics hub. An oil tanker in the Strait of Hormuz came under attack, and major oil companies and traders have suspended shipments through the strait entirely. Nine people were killed in Karachi when protesters stormed the US Consulate. China issued a formal condemnation, calling the killing a “serious violation of Iran’s sovereignty” and demanding an immediate halt to military operations.

The takeaway:

The succession vacuum is the story within the story. Khamenei’s death doesn’t end the conflict — it creates a power struggle inside Iran’s government while the bombs are still falling. There is no designated replacement, no obvious unifying figure, and a military still firing missiles. Meanwhile, the Strait of Hormuz — 15 million barrels of oil per day — is now an active conflict zone. Oil majors have already suspended shipments. The Economist put it plainly: this could be the biggest oil shock in years. When markets open Monday, expect a rough session.
