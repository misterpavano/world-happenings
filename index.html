Khamenei Is Dead — US & Israel Enter Day 2 of Iran Strikes

What happened:

Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, 86, was killed Saturday in a joint US-Israeli strike on his compound in Tehran — confirmed by Al Jazeera, Reuters, and CNBC. His daughter, son-in-law, and grandson were also killed. Iran has begun a 40-day mourning period and appointed Alireza Arafi as interim Supreme Leader. The strikes, now in their second day, have targeted nuclear sites, missile installations, and military infrastructure. Iran retaliated with a barrage of ballistic missiles at Israel and US bases across the Gulf.

The takeaway:

This is a watershed moment. A US-backed strike killed a sitting head of state of a major regional power — something that hasn't happened since Hussein's capture, and nothing like the elimination of a supreme leader. Trump called Khamenei's death "necessary." Oil markets are bracing for $100-a-barrel scenarios. The world just changed in a very permanent way.
